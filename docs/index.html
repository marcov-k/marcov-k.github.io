My Story

I was first exposed to coding by my dad via the Scratch platform when I was around 6 years old. Initially, we both worked in tandem while developing games, but over time I began to work more and more independently. I would create increasingly complex systems, even going as far as to code a simple enemy which would react to the player’s actions by the time I was 9 years old. At this point, my focus shifted to other areas, such as academics, before I once again returned to coding in 7th grade when my middle school had an opportunity for me to help develop a new school website. Here, I learned the basics of HTML and CSS through the Mozilla introductory course and documentation. The spring of the same school year, my dad gave me a course in game development using Unity and C#, at which point I finally became fully engrossed in the world of programming. Ever since then, I have continuously challenged myself to develop more and more complex software, ranging from video games to neural networks, physics simulations, and chemistry calculators. Finally, I recently returned to website development in order to dust off my skills from 7th grade and create my own portfolio website.

Why I Code

When I originally coded with Scratch, I found joy in simply making things on the screen move and react. However, as I have become increasingly involved in the complex areas of coding, particularly following my introduction to game development, the main attraction for me has become the problem solving aspect. I find immense satisfaction in finally perfecting a script or program to perform a complex task. The logical challenges provide a welcome change of pace compared to other activities I do on a daily basis, most of which are of an academic nature. Nonetheless, I have also retained the joy which I gained from my code coming to life on the screen, although now through a far wider range of actions which it may or may not perform. The feeling of seeing the computer perform tasks exactly the way I intended still remains indescribable.
